About Us

Your Neighbors, Your Fire Company

Port Matilda Fire Company #15 is an all-volunteer organization responding to structure fires, vehicle accidents, and mutual aid calls while members balance regular jobs and families.

Our primary coverage area spans Port Matilda Borough and parts of Halfmoon, Huston, Taylor, and Worth Townships. The company serves a large rural area with limited staffing resources and real equipment costs.

Municipal support covers only part of the budget. Fundraising and local giving keep the trucks moving, the gear maintained, and volunteers trained.

Stay Safe

Simple Safety Reminders

Keep the safety information practical and easy to scan.

Smoke Alarm Education

Smoke Detectors Save Lives

Test alarms monthly, change batteries regularly, and install detectors in bedrooms and on every floor.

Hands-On Fire Training

Know the P.A.S.S. Method

Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep. Keep an extinguisher accessible and make sure adults know how to use it.

Hydrant Access Saves Time

Keep Hydrants Clear

During winter storms, clear at least 3 feet around any hydrant near your property.

Plan, Practice, Protect

Practice an Escape Plan

Plan two exits from each room and choose a meeting spot outside the home.

Join the Team

We are actively recruiting both operational members and support volunteers. Training and role-specific equipment are provided where required.

Fire Company Support

Operational Support Roles

These positions strengthen day-to-day operations and help the company stay mission-ready.

Administration

Assist with grant filing, complete and submit required reports, and help coordinate non-emergency call response support.

Finance

Support basic bookkeeping, reporting, and financial recordkeeping workflows, including QuickBooks-based tasks.

Information Technology

Maintain security systems (camera and keypad access), support fire company computers, and apply updates to reporting and response systems.

Fundraising

Lead planning and execution of fundraising programs. Additional volunteer support in this area helps us restore critical events and revenue activities.

Building Operations and Maintenance

Assist with basic upkeep and minor repairs in non-garage spaces, including the kitchen, bathrooms, and recreation room.

Fire Services

Field Response Roles

Serve directly on incidents and community calls with training provided by the company.

Fireground Operations

Firefighter

Respond to building and wildland fires, traffic accidents, and emergency mutual-aid incidents. Members must be age 18+; junior members are welcome at age 14+.

Fire Police Traffic Control

Fire Police

Respond to fire and accident calls to direct traffic and control pedestrian traffic. Training and equipment is provided.

Apparatus Response Driver

Fire Truck Driver

Respond to calls and drive firefighting apparatus to fire and traffic-accident incidents.
